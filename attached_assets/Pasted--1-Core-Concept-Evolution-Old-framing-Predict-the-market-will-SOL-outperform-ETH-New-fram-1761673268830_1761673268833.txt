🎯 1. Core Concept Evolution

Old framing:

“Predict the market (will SOL outperform ETH).”
New framing:
“Predict the crowd (what will everyone else think, choose, or value).”

That tiny shift moves you from trading game → cultural forecasting game — something between Family Feud, Kahoot, and Polymarket-lite.

So Pally becomes a daily social guessing game about what the crowd believes will happen or prefers — not necessarily what actually happens.

🧠 2. Why Multiple-Choice Works

Most “prediction” games are binary (yes/no).
That gets boring fast.
Adding multiple choice unlocks:

Cultural questions (Which memecoin will moon next?)

Preference polls (Which chain feels hottest this week?)

Category battles (Which influencer will go viral next?)

Now the daily drop feels like a game show question, not a bet.

Examples:

Which token will trend most this week?
A) BONK B) DOGE C) WIF D) PEPE

Which project will announce something first?
A) ZKSync B) Blast C) LayerZero

Which meme describes 2025 the best?
A) UpOnly B) DownBad C) Sideways D) ai.eth

⚙️ 3. So Why Have 5 Votes per Day?

If you go this route, the “5 votes” mechanic isn’t random — it’s a design limiter to create scarcity and tension.
But let’s clarify its purpose:

Option A — Keep 5 votes

Each day might have multiple live questions (like Family Feud rounds).

You get 5 “Alpha Votes.”

You can spend them all on one question or spread them.

Once they’re gone, you wait until the next day.

This makes voting a resource you allocate strategically — adding a light layer of game depth.

Option B — Drop to 1 vote per question

Simplify it: you just answer today’s question once (the Wordle model).
If you want more engagement, you can introduce “Bonus Questions” or “Side Arenas.”

🏟️ 4. Multiple Question Types (Final Recommendation)

To keep it fun, you can rotate 3 main archetypes — each with slightly different scoring:

Type	Example	What it Tests	Scoring Idea
🧩 Consensus / Feud	“Which chain do you think most people will vote for?”	Social intuition	Points ∝ 1 / popularity (rarer picks = higher payout)
📈 Prediction / Reality	“Will BTC close above $80k?”	Market intuition	Binary win/loss after oracle result
🎭 Preference / Opinion	“Who’s the most iconic crypto founder?”	Cultural taste	Points for matching top 3 crowd picks

That mix makes each drop feel fresh.

Mondays → Market prediction

Wednesdays → Meme/Culture poll

Fridays → Big “Family Feud”-style showdown (multi-choice)

💎 5. Scoring Concept for “Family Feud” Mode

You could borrow Feud logic directly:

Step 1: Collect all votes (A/B/C/D).
Step 2: Reveal chart at close (e.g., A 50 %, B 30 %, C 15 %, D 5 %).
Step 3: Award points relative to popularity:

If you guessed top answer (A) → +100 pts.

If you guessed #2 (B) → +60 pts.

If you guessed #3 (C) → +30 pts.

If you guessed #4 (D) → +10 pts.

OR
Use a weighted rarity bonus (rarer picks get more if they win).

This creates that classic Feud payoff — “Show me… answer C!” — which is super satisfying in animation form.
